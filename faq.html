Common Questions

Here are answers to the most frequently asked questions about our Word to PDF converter. If you don't find what you're looking for, please contact us.

Is your Word to PDF converter free to use?

Yes, our service is completely free to use. You can convert unlimited Word documents to PDF without any charges or hidden fees. There's no registration required and no watermarks added to your PDF files.

What Word file formats do you support?

We support all common Microsoft Word formats including:

  • .doc (Word 97-2003 documents)
  • .docx (Word 2007 and later documents)
  • .rtf (Rich Text Format)
  • .odt (OpenDocument Text)
  • .txt (Plain text files)
Is there a file size limit for conversion?

Our free service currently supports files up to 50MB in size. For larger files, we recommend compressing images within your Word document before conversion or splitting the document into smaller parts.

How secure is my document during conversion?

We take your privacy seriously. All file conversions are performed securely in your browser (client-side) when possible. For larger files that require server processing, your files are automatically deleted from our servers within 1 hour of conversion.

Does the converter preserve formatting?

Yes, our converter maintains the original formatting of your Word document including:

  • Font styles and sizes
  • Paragraph spacing and alignment
  • Tables and columns
  • Images and charts
  • Headers and footers
  • Hyperlinks

For best results, use standard fonts that are available across different systems.

Can I convert multiple Word files at once?

Currently, our free version supports single file conversion. For batch processing of multiple files, you can convert them one by one or consider our premium service (coming soon) that will offer batch conversion capabilities.

Do you store my converted files?

No, we don't permanently store your original Word files or the converted PDFs. All files are processed temporarily during conversion and automatically deleted. The PDF is generated for immediate download only.

Why would my conversion fail?

Conversion might fail due to:

  • File size exceeding our limit
  • Corrupted Word document
  • Unsupported file format
  • Password-protected document
  • Network connectivity issues

Try again with a different file or contact us if the problem persists.

Still have questions?

We're here to help! Contact our support team for any additional questions.
