What Is Superscript Text?

Superscript text consists of characters that are positioned slightly above the baseline of normal text. These characters are smaller in size and are widely used to represent mathematical exponents, scientific notation, footnotes, and reference markers.

Examples of superscript text include expressions such as x², 10³, and reference numbers like ¹. Superscript formatting is especially important in academic and scientific communication where precision and clarity are critical.

Unlike visual formatting in word processors, a Unicode superscript generator produces actual Unicode characters. This means the superscript text can be copied and pasted anywhere without losing its formatting.

Superscript Letters and Numbers Explained

A common misconception is that every letter and number has a superscript equivalent. In reality, Unicode provides approximately 55–60 real superscript characters, including numbers, selected lowercase letters, some uppercase letters, and a few symbols.
